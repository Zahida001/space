Famous Astronauts in Space History

Yuri Gagarin

Yuri Gagarin, the first human to journey into space, is a Soviet hero who orbited Earth on April 12, 1961.

Mission Details:
  • Year: 1961
  • Spacecraft: Vostok 1
  • Mission Duration: 108 minutes

Neil Armstrong

Neil Armstrong made history as the first person to walk on the Moon during the Apollo 11 mission in 1969.

Mission Details:
  • Year: 1969
  • Spacecraft: Apollo 11
  • Mission Duration: 8 days, 14 hours, 12 minutes

Rakesh Sharma

India's first astronaut, Rakesh Sharma flew aboard the Soviet spacecraft Soyuz T-11 in 1984.

Mission Details:
  • Year: 1984
  • Spacecraft: Soyuz T-11
  • Mission Duration: 7 days, 21 hours, 40 minutes

Kalpana Chawla

Kalpana Chawla was the first woman of Indian origin in space, known for her two Space Shuttle missions.

Mission Details:
  • Years: 1997, 2003
  • Spacecraft: Space Shuttle Columbia
  • Mission Duration: 31 days, 14 hours, 54 minutes (total for both missions)

Sunita Williams

Sunita Williams holds the record for the most spacewalks by a woman, having spent 322 days in space.

Mission Details:
  • Years: 2006, 2007-2008
  • Spacecraft: Space Shuttle Discovery, ISS
  • Mission Duration: 322 days, 17 hours, 40 minutes

Sally Ride

Sally Ride was the first American woman in space, flying aboard STS-7 in 1983 on the Space Shuttle Challenger.

Mission Details:
  • Year: 1983
  • Spacecraft: Space Shuttle Challenger
  • Mission Duration: 6 days, 2 hours, 23 minutes

John Glenn

John Glenn became the first American to orbit the Earth and later returned to space aboard the Space Shuttle Discovery at age 77.

Mission Details:
  • Year: 1962, 1998
  • Spacecraft: Friendship 7, Space Shuttle Discovery
  • Mission Duration: 4 hours, 55 minutes (STS-95 flight)
